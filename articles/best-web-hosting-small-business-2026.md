## FAQ: Web Hosting for Small Business

### Q: Shared hosting vs. VPS — which do I need?
**A:** Start with **shared hosting** (all providers above offer it). It costs $3–$10/month and handles 10,000–50,000 monthly visitors easily. Upgrade to VPS ($20–$50/month) when you exceed 100,000 monthly visitors or need dedicated resources.

### Q: How much should I pay for small business hosting?
**A:** Expect to pay **$3–$5/month** for the first term (1–3 years), then **$8–$15/month** on renewal. Lock in the longest term you can afford to maximize savings.

### Q: Do I need managed WordPress hosting?
**A:** Only if you don't want to handle updates, security patches, and backups yourself. Bluehost and SiteGround both offer managed WordPress features on their shared plans — automatic updates, staging environments, and WordPress-specific caching.

### Q: Can I migrate my existing website to a new host?
**A:** Yes! Bluehost, SiteGround, and A2 Hosting offer **free migration services**. HostGator and DreamHost provide automated migration tools. The process typically takes 24–48 hours with zero downtime.

### Q: What happens when my intro pricing expires?
**A:** Your hosting renews at the standard rate (typically $8–$15/month). You can either pay the renewal or migrate to a new provider for another intro rate. Most hosts make migration easy with free tools.
