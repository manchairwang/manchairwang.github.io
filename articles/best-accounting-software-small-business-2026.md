## Accounting Software Buyer's Guide

### How to Choose the Right Accounting Software

1. **Assess your business type**
   - Service business? → FreshBooks or Xero
   - Product business? → QuickBooks Plus or Sage
   - Freelancer? → Wave or FreshBooks Lite

2. **Consider your team size**
   - Solo → Any option works
   - 2-5 users → Xero (unlimited users included)
   - 5+ users → Xero or QuickBooks Advanced

3. **Think about taxes**
   - US sales tax → QuickBooks or Xero
   - VAT/GST → Xero or Zoho Books
   - DIY taxes → Wave (free Schedule C)

4. **Plan for growth**
   - Stay micro → Wave or Zoho Free

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Can I switch accounting software later?
**Yes.** Most platforms offer data migration tools. QuickBooks and Xero both provide import wizards. Plan a transition during a slow period and keep your old account active for reference.

### Q: Do I need an accountant if I use accounting software?
**Not necessarily** — but it helps. Software handles data entry and categorization; an accountant handles strategy, tax optimization, and compliance. Many small businesses use software + a CPA for year-end only.

### Q: Is free accounting software safe?
**Yes.** Wave encrypts your data (256-bit SSL) and is PCI-DSS compliant. The trade-off is no phone support and limited features — but your data is secure.

### Q: Which software is easiest for beginners?
**FreshBooks** and **Wave** are the most beginner-friendly. QuickBooks has a steeper learning curve but offers more resources (tutorials, webinars, certified advisors).

### Q: Can I do payroll through these platforms?
QuickBooks, Gusto (via Xero integration), and Wave all offer payroll. Bench handles payroll as part of their service.

### Q: What if I have international clients?
**Xero** is the clear winner with 160+ currencies. QuickBooks offers multi-currency on Plus and above. FreshBooks handles multi-currency on Premium plans.
