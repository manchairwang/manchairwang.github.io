## FAQ

### Are free password managers safe?
Yes. Bitwarden's free tier uses the same encryption as its paid version. The main limitations are storage space and advanced features like dark web monitoring.

### Can password managers be hacked?
Password managers use zero-knowledge encryption — even the company cannot access your vault. Your master password is never stored or transmitted. The most common vulnerability is weak master passwords.

### Do password managers work on mobile?
All major password managers have iOS and Android apps with auto-fill support. Biometric unlock (Face ID, fingerprint) makes mobile usage seamless.

### What happens if I forget my master password?
Most password managers offer account recovery options: recovery codes (print and store safely), emergency contacts, or biometric recovery. Write down your recovery codes and store them in a safe place.

### Should I switch to passkeys?
Passkeys are more secure than passwords but adoption is still growing. The best approach is to use a password manager that supports both passwords and passkeys — 1Password and Bitwarden lead here.
