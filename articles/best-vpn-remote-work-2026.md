## FAQ: VPN for Remote Work

### Q: Is a free VPN safe for remote work?
**A:** Generally, no. Most free VPNs monetize through data collection or advertising. The exception is **Proton VPN's free tier**, which is genuinely no-logs and ad-free. For serious remote work, invest in a paid VPN.

### Q: Will a VPN slow down my internet for video calls?
**A:** A quality VPN like ExpressVPN or NordVPN adds minimal latency (5-15ms) on nearby servers. This won't affect Zoom or Teams calls. In fact, some users report *better* performance because their ISP stops throttling encrypted traffic.

### Q: Can my employer detect I'm using a VPN?
**A:** Yes, if they have network monitoring tools. However, most remote-friendly employers actually *encourage* VPN use. If your employer prohibits VPNs, check your company policy before using one.

### Q: Does a VPN protect against phishing emails?
**A:** No — a VPN encrypts your internet traffic but doesn't prevent phishing. NordVPN's Threat Protection Pro can block malicious websites, but always be cautious with email links.

### Q: Which VPN is best for accessing geo-blocked work resources?
**A:** NordVPN and ExpressVPN have the most reliable server networks for bypassing geo-restrictions. Both offer obfuscated servers that mask VPN traffic.
